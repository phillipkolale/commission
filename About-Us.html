Kenya is geo-strategically located at the confluence of the great lakes, Horn of Africa, and the Indian Ocean contiguity making it a gateway to Africa's major trading blocks such as the East Africa Community (EAC) and the Common Market for Eastern and Southern Africa (COMESA). It offers one of the best investment climates in the wider region with limitless Trade and Investment opportunities.Nairobi is a regional hub of economic and multilateral diplomacy. It hosts one of the four main United Nations centers and numerous international non-governmental organizations.We deliberately seek to engage with the vibrant Kenyan Diaspora who is Kenya's first-line ambassadors and partners in national development endeavors.

1. INTRODUCTION
Kenya and Botswana established bilateral relations immediately after Botswana achieved its independence in 1966. A consulate was opened in December 1997 following a request by the Kenyan Community in Botswana. The Consulate was upgraded to a fully-fledged High Commission in 2001. The core responsibility of the High Commission is to articulate Kenya’s foreign policy. The Mission has the mandate to implement Kenya’s foreign policy and strengthen bilateral relations with Botswana in particular, economic and commercial and safeguard Kenya’s national interests. The High Commission is also accredited by the Southern African Development Community (SADC).

To enhance this cooperation and ensure the effective and timely provision of services to its clients and stakeholders in pursuit of its mandate, the High Commission has developed a Service Charter. 

This Service Charter defines the Mission’s mandate to our clients and our commitment and desire to enhance service delivery. The Charter further establishes the standards of our services, redresses mechanisms where our services fall short of expectations, and expresses our commitment to the delivery of quality services in pursuit of our customers’ satisfaction. This Service Charter also provides insights into our core functions, values, and our commitment to continuous improvement. The Mission is committed to periodic evaluation of its performance based on the implementation of the Service Charter.

9. OUR SERVICE STANDARDS
We are committed to providing the highest standards of services to all our customers. You can expect the following from us:
Quality
We will:
Treat you with respect and courtesy, maintaining confidentiality as required;
Identify ourselves when we speak to you;
Be clear and helpful in our interactions;
Act with care, diligence, honesty and integrity as we  respond and deal with your issues;
Refer enquiries we cannot answer to an appropriate agency/authority;

Responsiveness
We will Endeavour to:
Deal with your enquiries and complaints quickly and effectively;
Answer your phone calls promptly;
 Attend to visitors promptly upon arrival;
Notify you of our meetings, at least five days in advance;
Reply to your letters, faxes and e-mails within five (5) working days and on more complex issues, our initial reply will give you an estimate of the time a full response will take, and the cost, if any;
Payment for goods, services and works within two days upon submission of accurate invoices and any other supporting documents in line with Government procurement rules and regulations.

Accessibility
We will be available:
From 8.00 a.m. to 4.30 p.m. from Monday to Thursday and from 8.00 am to 2.00pm on Fridays.
We are closed on public holidays and have a lunch hour break between 1.00 and 2.00 p.m. from Mondays to Thursdays.

Service improvement
We aim to:
Ensure that the accuracy and quality of our services remain world-class;
Improve procedures for monitoring the quality of our services and reporting the results;
Upgrade the ways in which we deliver our services, in line with increasing improvements in technology and the changing needs of our clients;
Ensure that our Website is interactive and user-friendly; and
We will consider all suggestions fully and promptly in our planning for service improvement.
